Homework 1¶

Nearly 1.3 million people die in automobile crashes in the US every year. Speeding is cited as a major factor in approximately 31% of all fatalities. Automated Traffic Enforcement (ATE) devices hold the promise to be an effective deterrent and save lives. These devices automatically monitor traffic flow, provide immediate feedback to drivers and - when necessary - are able to capture data and issue citations to motorists.

Each unit is equipped with a speed sensor (radar), a display, and a camera. Your job is to implement the module responsible for automatic ticketing. When a car approaches the device, the sensor detects the speed of the vehicle. When your module determines that a violation has occurred, it calculates the details of the citation and forwards it to the Department of transportation. Broadly this process looks like this:

The speed limit for the unit is set by an operator when the device is installed. The citation is a message determined by the speed.

Speed Over Limit Message
Up to 3mph Compliant
3mph - 8mph Warning
Over 8mph Fine

When a fine is issued, the amount is calculated as \$50 + \\$5 for each mph over the limit, with a cap at \$200. Fines are doubled if you are in a construction zone (for a max of \\$400).

def generate_citation(ID, speed, speedLimit, construction):
    print(str(ID), end = "")
    speed -= speedLimit
    if speed < 3:
        print(" Compliant")
    elif 3 <= speed < 8:
        print(" Warning")
    elif speed >= 8:
        Fine = min((50 + speed*5), 200)*(1 + int(construction))
        print(" Fine $" + str(Fine))
